It was believed that this awareness would be preferable for all of us. Since we can all do our part to prevent global warming and protect the environment. We can help solve the problem and affect change by making decisions that have less of a negative impact on the environment.

BETTER FOR A CHANGE

Start with these ten impactful actions!

The amount of greenhouse gases emitted as a result of a person's activity is referred to as their

"carbon footprint."

SAVE ENERGY AT HOME

Coal, oil, and gas are major sources of our country's electricity and heat. Reduce your heating and cooling, use energy-efficient electric appliances like LED lights and washers, wash your clothes in cold water, and hang up your laundry to dry rather than using a dryer. Your carbon footprint can be decreased by up to 900 kilos of CO2e per year by increasing the energy efficiency of your home, for as by installing greater insulation or switching from an oil or gas furnace to an electric heat pump.

WALK, BIKE OR TAKE TRANSPORT

Driving less and using a bike or walking will both improve your health and fitness while reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Consider using a bus or train for farther distances. Comparing a car-dependent lifestyle to one without one can help you cut your annual carbon footprint by up to 2 tons of CO2.

EAT MORE VEGETABLES

Plant-based food supply generally uses less energy, water, and land and produces fewer greenhouse gas emissions. Up to 500 kilos of CO2e can be removed from the atmosphere each year by changing from a mixed to a vegetarian diet (or up to 900 kilograms for a vegan diet).

CONSIDER YOUR TRAVEL

Fossil fuels are used extensively in airplanes, which results in enormous greenhouse gas emissions. As a result, reducing on your travel is among the quickest methods to lessen your impact on the environment. If at all possible, meet digitally, commute by road, or cancel the long trip altogether. Your carbon footprint can be decreased by up to 2 tons by taking fewer long-distance return flights.

THROW AWAY LESS FOOD

When food is wasted, the energy and resources necessary to grow, produce, package, and transport it are also wasted. Also, methane, a strong greenhouse gas, is produced when food breaks in a landfill. Use the items you purchase, then compost the excess. Your carbon footprint can be decreased by up to 300 kilos of CO2e per year by cutting back on food waste.

REDUCE,REUSE, RECYLCE & REPAIR

At every stage of production, from the gathering of raw materials through the manufacture and delivery of commodities to goods we purchase produce carbon emissions. Buy fewer stuff, do secondhand shopping, try to fix what you can, and recycle to help the environment. A kilogram of textile production produces 17 kilos of CO2 equivalent.

CHANGE YOUR SOURCE OF ENERGY

Learn first whether gas, coal, or oil is used to power your home. Check to see if you can convert to renewable sources like wind or solar, if it's possible. Alternately, you might put solar panels on your roof to provide electricity for your house. By switching to renewable energy sources like wind or solar for your home instead of fossil fuels like coal, oil, or gas, you can cut your carbon emissions by up to 1.5 tons annually.

SWITCH TO AN ELECTRONIC VEHICLE

If you intend to purchase a vehicle, think about going electric as more and more affordable models hit the market. Electric cars produce much less emissions of greenhouse gases than gas or diesel-powered vehicles, which helps to reduce air pollution in many nations. Your carbon footprint can be decreased by up to 2 tons per year by switching from a gasoline or diesel-powered car to an electric vehicle.

MAKE YOUR MONEY COUNT

The environment is impacted by everything we buy. You have the authority to decide whatever products and services you favor. Make purchases from businesses who are committed to reducing their waste and gas emissions and who utilize resources ethically to lessen their impact on the environment. Your carbon footprint can be significantly reduced by making sure your savings are put into environmentally friendly enterprises.

SPEAK UP

Speak up and encourage others to take action with you. One of the simplest and fastest methods to change things is to do it.
